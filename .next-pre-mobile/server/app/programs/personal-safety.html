PERSONAL SAFETY TRAINING / FOUNDATION MODULE

The bedrockof everywoman’s safety.

Workshops and curriculum covering situational awareness, instinct, secure movement, and personal protection. Built on twenty years of expertise. Designed for the women who already know they can save themselves — and want the tools to do it well.

Curriculum

Four areas.
One foundation.

01

Situational Awareness

Look up, look down, look around. Train your attention to read environments before they read you. Learn what your eyes should be doing in any space — public, private, familiar, unfamiliar.

02

Instinct & Intuition

Your sixth sense is data. We will teach you how to listen to it, trust it, and act on it without second-guessing — and when something feels off, how to leave.

03

Secure Movement

How to walk, where to walk, when to walk. Visibility, route planning, headphone protocols, and the practical mechanics of moving through public space safely.

04

Personal Protection

Personal alarms, situational deterrents, and the principles behind effective protection. Evidence-based protocols, not theatrics. What actually works, what does not, and why.

Designed for

Women at every stage.

01

University students

Newly independent, navigating campuses, hostels, late nights, and unfamiliar cities for the first time.

02

Working professionals

Daily commutes, business travel, evening meetings, and the realities of moving through cities for work.

03

Mothers and caregivers

Protecting yourself while protecting others. Modeling safety habits for the daughters and sisters watching you.

Customized for age, profession, and context. No two cohorts run identically.

Our approach

Real protocols.
Real practice.

Personal safety training is full of bad advice — outdated assumptions, performative defense moves, and fear-based marketing. SafeHers teaches what actually works: protocols rooted in two decades of national security work, adapted for the realities African women face. We do not sell fear. We build capability.

Common questions

Before you sign up.

When does the workshop run?

We are confirming launch dates for the founding cohort. Joining the waitlist puts you first in line for date announcements.

What does it cost?

The founding cohort is offered free of charge. Future cohorts will be priced affordably, with scholarships available for students.

Do I need any prior experience?

No. The curriculum is built for women who know they need this knowledge but have never been formally taught.

What is the time commitment?

Half a day for in-person Accra workshops. Three two-hour sessions across two weeks for virtual cohorts.

Can my organization book a private cohort?

Yes. Universities, NGOs, and corporations can book private cohorts of 30 or more participants. Reach out via the contact page.
